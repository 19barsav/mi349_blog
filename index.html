Is Your DEI Training Lacking?

By Savvy B.
06/01/2022

Good day, readers!

I started a new position in February. It is at a Michigan based healthcare non-profit, and I have enjoyed every second of working for them. Unsurprisingly, I was required to complete training upon my hiring. The training was okay, but the DEI training was really lacking in quite a few areas. In some instances, the guidance was more dangerous than it was useful. Below is how I recognized it, and what I did to bring change. I hope it inspires others to speak up in their workplace!

Recognizing It:

  1. Are the steps confusing?
  2. Do the steps contradict what you've been told before?
  3. Can you think of a situation where this step would be dangerous

In my case, one of the steps was to approach a transgender coworker and ask them if you could ask them questions privately. First, asking to ask them questions privately is not private, and doesn't seem work appropriate. Second, many people of minority groups will say ‚Äúyes‚Äù to remain compliant and reflect conflict. Approaching them seems to contradict what folks want, and that's to be left alone. Third, asking someone to speak privately puts them in an uncomfortable and dangerous situation where they have no witnesses if something doesn't go wrong. It is nice to believe that everyone has good intentions, but they don't.

How To Fix It:

  1. Recognize that it isn't your job (unless it is!)
  2. Tell your immediate boss/mentor (optional)
  3. Speak to someone from the DEI office/ human resources
  4. Follow-up in a few months to make sure THEY did THEIR job!

Hope this helps!
